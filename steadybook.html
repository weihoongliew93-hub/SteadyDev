FAQ

Common questions

What kind of businesses is SteadyBook for?

Anything booked by time slot: sports courts and simulator bays, studios and treatment rooms, restaurant tables, equipment or gear rental, salon chairs, meeting rooms and more. You define the resources, prices and hours.

Is there a free trial?

Yes — 14 days free on the Starter plan, with no credit card required. You get the complete plugin with every feature unlocked, not a limited demo, so you can set up your real resources and take real bookings while you decide. If you do nothing at the end of the 14 days, it simply stops — you're never charged. Professional and Agency don't include a trial, but you can start on Starter and upgrade once you're happy.

What do I actually receive after buying?

Immediately after checkout you get the plugin ZIP to download plus a license key, both shown on the confirmation page and emailed to you. You upload the ZIP to WordPress and activate it with the key.

What does the license cover?

Every feature of the plugin, plus updates & support while your subscription is active — on as many sites as your plan allows (1, 5 or 25). Updates install automatically inside your WordPress dashboard.

Which plan do I need?

All plans unlock the exact same plugin — the only difference is how many sites you can activate on. Starter ($299/year) covers one business website. Professional ($699/year) covers 5 sites at $140 each. Agency ($1,499/year) covers 25 client sites at $60 each. Need more than 25? Get in touch and we'll size a plan to your site count. You can upgrade at any time and only pay the difference.

Do staging and development sites use up a license?

No. Staging, development and local installs (for example staging.yoursite.com or yoursite.local) don't count towards your site limit — only live production sites do.

Can I pay monthly?

All plans are billed yearly. If you'd like to try SteadyBook before committing, start the free 14-day trial instead — no credit card, every feature unlocked, and nothing to cancel if you decide it isn't for you.

What are the requirements?

WordPress 5.0+ and PHP 7.2+. For approval emails to land reliably we recommend a simple SMTP plugin (e.g. WP Mail SMTP) — this is standard for any WordPress site sending mail.

What happens if I don't renew?

Your existing bookings and data stay in WordPress. You simply stop receiving new updates and support until you renew.
